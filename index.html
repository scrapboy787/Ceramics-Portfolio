This is a container I made to store some niter/saltpetre/potassium nitrate. It is glazed with a poo bear colour glaze as a base, and tuxedo black glaze for the symbol, both are Stroke & Coat brand glaze.

This is not entirely my project. A friend made it but when it was fired some other unglazed piece exploded and got shraptnil stuck to it, so she gave it to me. I sanded and filed down the shraptnil and glazed over it with Tuxedo black from Stroke&coat. Then I put the symbol for charcoal compound on the sides of it with Cotton Tail White glaze also Stroke&coat brand. I intend on using this container to store charcoal compound in it.

This is a plate I made as a decoration to be attached to my appothacary cabnet. It is a unicorn horn it reprezents an appothacary work and expertiese because of the myth that a unicorn can heal any injury even death. Unfortionaly it warped during drying, and even more while being fired in the kiln. I Glazed it with the Obsidian Jungle Gem glaze and filled in the symbol with a gold NEWELL brand paint pen.

This is a piece I made while at EK's Art Barn. It's a pumpkin that I spent a okay amount of effort and time into. The back of it exploded and the stem came off while firing. I glued the stem back together and reattached it, but sa for the back side there is nothing I can do to fix it. The kind folks at EK,s Art Barn glazed it for me, so I dont know exaxtly what glaze they used.

This is a plate I made using a technique called mishima. I made it to collect the water that comes out of the botton of a pot with a christmas cactus in it. I formed the lip without a mold, I waited until it was leather hard then pinched and bent the edge. It's glazed with a cotton tail white and peri twinkle blue glazes from Stroke&Coat.

This is a container I made to store some Aqua Tofana/Arsenic Trioxide. It is made with a coil technique and then covered up with some more clay. The lid had to be made twice because the first one didn't fit. The inside is glazed with a mixture of candy apple red and tuxedo black, the outside is just tuxedo black. the symbol on the lid is done with a gold paint pen from NEWELL, and then covered up with clear glue to seal it and pervent the gold from wairing off.
